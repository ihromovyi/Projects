# Milan Airbnb — Geography & Regulation

A 4-person Business Intelligence project on ~23,000 Milan Airbnb listings (June & September scrapes).
**This is my section — Geography & Regulation.** (Teammates covered Pricing, Host Profiles, Guest Experience.)

**Questions:** Where is supply concentrated, and which neighbourhoods balance demand and price? What is the licensing-compliance picture — and is a license a competitive advantage?

**Key findings:**
- Supply concentrates in the historic centre (Duomo, Brera), but **density ≠ demand** — Centrale outperforms Duomo (**2.92 vs 1.94** reviews/month) at **€177 vs €381** (transit accessibility beats prestige).
- A large **gray market**: 6,600+ listings unlicensed; the centre is compliant, the outskirts drop **below 60%**.
- Licensed listings hold higher review scores (**4.7+**) — a license is a quality signal.
