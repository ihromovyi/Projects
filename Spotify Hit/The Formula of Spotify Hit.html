Introduction

I asked the core question: What external factors give a song the most chances for popularity if they exist?

I picked a dataset with recent and contemporary Spotify tunes (mainly from 2025) - data for each song in 2025. A single track, together with its artist, album, and popularity data (from 0 to 100 ), is represented by each row. I will evaluate Artist's Popularity, Genre, Track Duration, Year of release, Explicit/Clean language type, Type of album, which at final will show me the ideal metric formula for popular song in 2025.

Popularity of the track - is the most important metric, which I evaluate - it is calculated by Spotify algorithms mainly from the recent listenings, so it makes sense only now - after the data was collected (month ago).

Context & Question

Design Decisions

Reflection

The formula for track success in 2025:

1. The earlier the song is released, the more chances for the gaining popularity

2. Release song in album, not a single

3. Use Explicit language

4. Reaggation, k-pop, alternative pop, and r&b are preferrable genres

5. Track duration in range of 3 to 4 mintues

6. You would rather be a popular artist

Finally we I can't say that with external factors the track would be popular. We have seen that even popular artists don't have popular tracks. So this analysis finally tells us more about the song market than about the formula for popular song.
